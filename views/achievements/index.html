The Assiut Robotics

Achievements

ROV ("Remotely Operated Vehicle") 2017 Competition

The 2017 ROV Competition, organized by the Marine Advanced Technology Education (MATE) Center, was a prestigious international event focused on promoting innovation in underwater robotics. Teams from around the world participated, showcasing their skills in designing, building, and operating ROVs to complete a series of complex underwater tasks. The competition aimed to inspire students to pursue careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) by providing a real-world context for learning and collaboration.

Minesweepers Competition

The Minesweepers Competition is an international event aimed at developing advanced robotic solutions for the detection and clearance of landmines and unexploded ordnances. This competition encourages innovation and collaboration among teams from around the world, focusing on creating safe, effective, and efficient robotic systems that can operate in challenging environments. By bringing together participants with diverse skills in robotics, electronics, and software engineering, the Minesweepers Competition plays a crucial role in advancing technology for humanitarian demining efforts.
